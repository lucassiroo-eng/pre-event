# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Factorial sales leadership and strategy team (Lucas + Jonathan, eventually VP Sales). Reviewing the Spain commercial funnel in a meeting room or on a screen share with management. Primary task on any given screen: identify which segments have the best conversion rates, where untapped opportunity exists (SASOR companies not yet in HubSpot), and where to focus sales resources. Users are data-literate but time-constrained; they scan, then drill.

## Product Purpose

Internal analysis dashboard for Spain market strategy. Ingests HubSpot pipeline data (49k companies) and the national company registry (SASOR, 95k companies) to show: funnel conversion by segment (region, size, industry, provenance), CMRR distribution, and whitespace between the total addressable market and current penetration. Success: a sales director can walk into a board meeting, open this tab, filter to a region or segment, and confidently answer "where are we winning and where are we leaving money on the table."

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, insider. This is a tool that earns trust through data clarity, not decoration. Tone: analytical report, not SaaS marketing.

## Anti-references

- Generic BI dashboard aesthetics (Tableau/Power BI defaults: blue bars everywhere, rainbow pie charts)
- Over-decorated "hero metric" cards with gradient accents and glow effects
- Consumer-app warmth (rounded everything, pastel backgrounds, emoji labels)
- Dark mode: wrong register for a daytime management presentation that may be printed or screenshared

## Design Principles

1. **Data first, chrome second.** Every visual element earns its place by making the data easier to read or act on. No decoration that doesn't encode meaning.
2. **Hierarchy of insight.** The eye should land on conversion rates and anomalies, not on labels or chrome. High-opportunity cells should pop without the user hunting.
3. **Scannable at a glance.** Management doesn't read tables; they scan for outliers. Color, scale, and layout must make outliers obvious before the user consciously looks for them.
4. **Printable clarity.** The palette and contrast must survive a projector, a screenshare at low resolution, and a printed handout.
5. **Trust through precision.** Exact numbers, consistent rounding, no unexplained Others blobs. If data is missing, say so explicitly rather than hiding it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Color coding always paired with numeric labels (never color alone for meaning). Sufficient contrast on all rate cells at normal zoom.
